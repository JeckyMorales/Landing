Tawi-Tawi Province

Welcome to Tawi-Tawi Province

HISTORY

Tawi-Tawi is the southernmost province of the Philippines, located in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). It is an archipelago composed of around 107 islands and islets, with Bongao as its capital.

The history of Tawi-Tawi is rich and diverse, influenced by various cultures and civilizations throughout the centuries. It has been inhabited by indigenous peoples, including the Sama-Bajau, Tausug, and Badjao, who have maintained their unique traditions and way of life.

Throughout history, Tawi-Tawi has been a center of trade and maritime activities, with its strategic location along major sea routes in the Sulu Sea. It has also been influenced by Islamic civilization, particularly through the spread of Islam in the region.

Today, Tawi-Tawi is known for its stunning natural beauty, pristine beaches, and vibrant marine biodiversity. It attracts tourists seeking adventure and exploration, offering opportunities for diving, snorkeling, and island hopping.

ECONOMY

Seaweeds industry is at the heart of Tawi-Tawi's aquaculture sector which produces 294,595 metric tons per year. In 2021, the Philippines produced around 1.3 million metric tons of seaweeds, wherein 87% is produced by the provinces within Mindanao and Palawan, including Tawi-Tawi.

DEMOGRAPHICS

The Sama-Bajau are the dominant ethnic group of the islands of Tawi-Tawi. They are also found in other islands of the Sulu Archipelago, coastal areas of Mindanao and other islands in the northern Philippines; as well as northern and eastern Borneo, Sulawesi, and throughout the eastern Indonesian islands.

BIODIVERSITY

Tawi-Tawi is known for its rich marine biodiversity. The province is home to diverse coral reef ecosystems, seagrass beds, and mangrove forests. These habitats support a wide array of marine species including various fish, invertebrates, marine mammals, and sea turtles.

GEOGRAPHY

Tawi-Tawi is composed of around 307 islands and islets scattered across the Sulu Sea and Celebes Sea. The main islands include Tawi-Tawi Island (also known as Bongao Island), Sanga-Sanga Island, and Panglima Sugala (Balimbing) Island. Other significant islands include Sibutu, Sitangkai, and Turtle Islands.

SCHOOL UNIVERSITIES

Tawi-Tawi College of Technology and Oceanography (MSU-TCTO): This is a satellite campus of the Mindanao State University system located in Tawi-Tawi. It offers undergraduate programs in various fields such as engineering, marine sciences, education, and business administration.
